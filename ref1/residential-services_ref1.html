Common Reasons Homes Are Overassessed

Many Cook County homeowners are surprised to learn that property assessments may not always reflect true market value. Common causes of over-assessment include:

Incorrect Property Records

Public records may contain inaccurate information such as square footage, number of rooms, or building characteristics.

Comparable Homes Are Assessed Lower

Homes with similar features in the same neighborhood may have lower assessed values.

Market Value Declines

Housing market fluctuations may cause a property's market value to fall below its assessed value.

Property Condition Issues

Structural problems, outdated features, or deferred maintenance can reduce a home's value.

Automated Valuation Errors

Assessment models used by the county may not fully reflect unique property characteristics.

If any of these situations apply to your home, you may qualify for a Cook County residential property tax appeal.

We have developed a streamlined process that allows homeowners to start an appeal quickly while ensuring every case is supported by strong evidence.

1

Free Property Assessment Review

We analyze your property's assessed value and compare it to similar homes in your area.

2

Comparable Sales Analysis

Our team reviews recent market sales to determine whether your property has been overvalued.

3

Evidence Preparation

We compile supporting documentation including comparable properties, market data, and assessment discrepancies.

4

Filing the Appeal

Our attorneys file the appeal with the appropriate authority before the township deadline.

5

Representation Before Appeal Boards

We represent homeowners before:

  • Cook County Assessor's Office

Reduction in Assessed Value

If the appeal is successful, the assessed value of the property is reduced, lowering the tax bill.
